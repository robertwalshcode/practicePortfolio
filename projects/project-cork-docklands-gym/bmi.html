Why you should know your BMI?

BMI, or Body Mass Index, is a key indicator of your overall health and helps assess whether you are at a healthy weight based on your height. It provides an estimate of body fat and potential health risks. While it is not a perfect measure since it doesn't distinguish between muscle mass and fat mass, it does give you a general idea of where you stand in terms of health. Maintaining a healthy BMI is important because being overweight or underweight can increase the risk of various health conditions such as heart disease, diabetes, and high blood pressure. By keeping track of your BMI and making adjustments to your lifestyle if needed, you can lower your health risks and improve your overall well-being.
